Bio

I am a Computer Science Ph.D. student at Ohio State University, specializing in operating systems, security, and architecture. With a strong background in Computer Science and Mathematics from Wittenberg University, I am passionate about exploring the intersection of theory and application.

My current research focuses on designing and implementing distributed low power systems for use in agriculture. I am interested in leveraging emerging technologies to address the challenges of sustainable farming and improve crop yield through intelligent automation and data-driven decision-making.

Throughout my academic journey, I have collaborated on various projects involving embedded systems, machine learning, and cybersecurity. I believe in the power of interdisciplinary collaboration and constantly seek opportunities to contribute to cutting-edge research and innovation.
